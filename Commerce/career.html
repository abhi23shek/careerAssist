Best Career Options in Commerce After 12th

With Maths

Among all the students who have chosen commerce as a stream in their 12th have two options to choose from. The first one is doing their 12th in Commerce with Maths as an extra paper. Choosing maths as an extra paper in 12th commerce opens a lot of options for them in their higher education. The below-listed career opportunities do not certainly require maths as an extra paper in 12th commerce but are more suitable for individuals who have chosen maths in their 12th commerce.

  • Chartered Accountant (CA): Accountancy may be your calling if you are analytical, have great reasoning skills, and are good with numbers. As a chartered accountant, you can take on a wide range of duties and responsibilities, including financial analysis and reporting, budget management, review and auditing, tax counselling, and so on.
  • Investment Banker: For many years, investment banking has been one of the most sought-after careers. This is a high-risk job that requires a significant time commitment. You will be assisting a wide range of clients, from governments to enterprises and organizations, in raising funds and completing mergers and acquisitions.
  • Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA): A chartered financial analyst (CFA) is one of the most prestigious career possibilities in finance, and the certification is recognized globally once granted by the CFA institute to the candidate. To become a charter holder, candidates must pass three extremely rigorous exams, have a bachelor’s degree, and have at least four years of relevant job experience.
  • Cost Accountant: As a cost accountant, you will be responsible for tasks such as cost estimation, budgeting, collection of data, setting up real-time inventory numbers, and so on. In a nutshell, you will assist businesses in ensuring that their money is well-planned and worthwhile by spreading cost-efficiency.
  • Company Secretary (CS): A company secretary’s job might entail critical activities like counselling the board on legal, statutory, and regulatory requirements, as well as preparing corporate tax filings. The finest aspect of this career path is that any student from any stream can apply for the foundation program after passing their 12th-grade exams and passing the three phases of exams. This is certainly one of the great courses after 12th Commerce that pays well.
  • Research Analyst: One can want to be a research analyst in many fields, such as operations, economics, finance, and stock. You need a bachelor’s degree in a specific area of study, like economics, marketing, or finance, to work in this field. However, after a few years of experience, a Master of Business Administration (MBA) course can also help your career.
  • Financial Advisor: Personal financial advisors are professionals in the field of finance who provide assistance to individuals in achieving their financial objectives, both in the short-term and long-term. The advice provided by professionals can encompass a wide array of topics, including property investments, taxation, insurance, and retirement planning. In order to achieve success, personal financial advisors must possess an in-depth knowledge of investment markets and demonstrate a knack for identifying optimal stocks, bonds, or funds for their individual customers.
  • Actuary: Individuals who possess a strong aptitude for mathematics, statistics, and business management possess the potential to readily enter this particular area and generate significant value within the realm of business. The daily tasks may encompass the application of financial modelling techniques and risk analysis methodologies to evaluate potential property loss, disability, or other foreseeable challenges that could potentially affect the organization. The profession of actuary is widely regarded as a highly esteemed vocation within the field of commerce, offering a multitude of opportunities for individuals seeking a diversified range of professional experiences. Moreover, actuarial work provides practitioners with the advantageous option of pursuing either independent endeavours or affiliating themselves with well-established organizations, so affording them a considerable degree of flexibility in their career paths.
  • Cost and Management Accountant (CMA): CMA is an internationally recognized professional certification for accounting and business professionals. By enhancing the four pillars of expertise — Strategy, Management, Regulatory Framework, and Financial reporting — it prepares commerce students to become business executives of the future. As a CMA, one is required to provide professional services in investment planning, profit management, and the overall managerial decision-making process in service and manufacturing industries.
  • Data Scientist: Data science is an excellent career option for mathematics majors because it involves strong problem-solving skills. The demand for individuals with data science expertise has increased significantly over the past few years. It is a relatively new and lucrative discipline.

Without Maths

The second option is doing their 12th in Commerce without Maths as an extra paper. The majority of options are still available for all the individuals who have not chosen maths as an extra paper in their 12th commerce. The below-listed career opportunities are not only for individuals who have maths as an extra paper in 12th commerce but are more suitable for individuals who have not chosen maths in their 12th commerce.

  • Marketing: After completing the twelfth grade, commerce students can seek entry-level marketing positions and opportunities to acquire experience to eventually advance in their field. There are numerous marketing positions available with small businesses, particularly in the field of digital marketing, which is on the rise. An MBA in marketing is also helpful to have a rock solid career in marketing after doing graduation.
  • Journalism and Mass Communication: Students may elect to pursue this career path, which teaches them about the nation’s most recent economic developments. After completing this course, candidates can readily find employment in a variety of print and digital media channels in the country and abroad.
  • Hotel Management: Hotel management is another of the highest-paying professions for business students. Hotels play an important role in travel and tourism and have a substantial impact on national and international economies. One of the most sought-after specializations for commerce students offers many of the highest-paying positions, with or without math.
  • Entrepreneur: Entrepreneurship is one of the exciting job prospects for commerce without mathematics. Entrepreneurship entails a variety of processes, from the conceiving of the business idea to its actualisation. Starting a business from the beginning is extremely difficult. To be a successful entrepreneur, a person must be extremely active, and risk-taking, possess leadership abilities, and be creative. It is one of the most challenging yet rewarding career options available to Commerce majors without a math background.
  • Psychologist: The discipline of psychology is concerned with comprehending the human psyche and how it influences behaviour. A psychologist diagnoses and treats a wide range of mental, emotional, and behavioural disorders. Some psychologists work in settings such as schools/universities, academic institutions, and research facilities, while others work in hospitals, corporations, rehabilitation centres, and private practice. These career options increase the variety of options and scope for Commerce without Maths after 12th grade.
  • Fashion Designing: With the expansion of education in India, various new fashion design courses have emerged. These are not fundamental courses, but rather specialize in textile, jewellery, and footwear design, among others. A bachelor’s degree in fashion designing is required to become a fashion designer. You can enrol in this course after completing 10+2. Many institutions offer a variety of short-term courses designed specifically for students with advanced degrees in fields other than fashion design who are interested in taking fashion design courses.
  • Teaching: Teaching subjects that do not involve mathematics may not require a mathematics background, and this is a very rewarding career option that allows you to engage with students and influence their futures. The opportunity to contribute to society is an added benefit of this line of work, and students aspiring to become teachers can pursue bachelor’s degrees in humanities, commerce and business.
  • Travel and Tourism: Tourism is an industry that has experienced significant growth since the advent of globalisation, and employment prospects and opportunities in the travel and tourism sectors have increased proportionally. Graduates with skills are in high demand in the tourism industry and related disciplines, making tourism jobs lucrative and promising for advancement.
  • Foreign Trade: To study foreign trade, students can opt for a bachelor’s in Foreign Trade (BFT). The BFT course emphasizes on aspects and factors that influence international commerce and market forces. This course examines the fundamentals of logistics, supply chain management, export, import, and trade policies. Candidates who finish the course can find employment opportunities in both the public and private sectors.
  • Lawyer: Students without math skills can become lawyers. To pursue a career in the legal field, an LLB degree is essential. In India, a Bachelor of Law is required for admission to a legal practice court. If you master interpersonal ability, analytical skills, and a comprehensive comprehension of legal principles, becoming a lawyer becomes more accessible for business students. A lawyer’s duties include providing legal advice, drafting legal documents, and advising clients on wills, contracts, and real estate transactions.
