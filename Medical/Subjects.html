Subjects in 11th Class

Physics

Physics is fundamental to understanding the natural world. It encompasses a broad range of topics that explain the laws governing matter and energy. Key areas of study include:

  • Motion: Understanding how objects move and interact with forces.
  • Units and Measurements: Learning to measure and quantify physical properties.
  • Friction: Exploring the resistance between surfaces in contact.
  • Center of Mass: Analyzing the point where mass is considered to be concentrated.
  • Moment of Inertia: Studying rotational motion and resistance to changes in motion.
  • Elastic Behavior: Investigating how materials deform and return to their original shape.
  • Thermal Equilibrium: Understanding how heat is transferred and equilibrated in systems.
  • Heat, Work, and Internal Energy: Examining the concepts of energy transfer and transformations.

Chemistry

Chemistry explores the composition, structure, properties, and reactions of matter. It is crucial for various fields, including medicine, engineering, and environmental science. Main topics include:

  • Chemical Reactions: Learning how substances interact and transform.
  • Periodic Table: Understanding elements and their properties.
  • Organic Chemistry: Studying carbon-based compounds and their reactions.
  • Inorganic Chemistry: Exploring non-carbon compounds and their properties.
  • Physical Chemistry: Investigating the physical aspects of chemical systems.
  • Analytical Chemistry: Techniques for identifying and quantifying substances.

English

English is essential for communication and is often a requirement for various academic and professional pursuits. Key areas include:

  • Comprehension: Understanding and interpreting written texts.
  • Grammar: Mastering the rules of language structure.
  • Essay Writing: Developing coherent arguments and expressing ideas effectively.
  • Unseen Passages: Analyzing and interpreting new texts.
  • Vocabulary: Expanding and utilizing a broad range of words.

Biology

Biology examines the living world and its interactions. It covers a wide range of topics relevant to various medical and scientific fields. Main subjects include:

  • Cell Biology: Understanding cell structure and function.
  • Genetics: Exploring heredity, genes, and genetic disorders.
  • Human Anatomy: Studying the structure and function of the human body.
  • Physiology: Investigating how biological systems function.
  • Ecology: Examining interactions between organisms and their environment.
  • Evolution: Understanding the processes driving the diversity of life.
