Career and Degree Options After Humanities Students

Degree Options after Humanities

Bachelor of Arts (BA)

The Bachelor of Arts (BA) is one of the most common degrees pursued by humanities students. It offers a wide range of specializations such as History, Political Science, Sociology, Psychology, English, and more. This degree provides a strong foundation in critical thinking, writing, and analysis, and opens up diverse career opportunities in fields like education, public administration, journalism, and more.

Bachelor of Fine Arts (BFA)

For students with a passion for creative arts, a Bachelor of Fine Arts (BFA) is an ideal choice. This degree focuses on visual and performing arts such as painting, sculpture, music, dance, and theater. BFA graduates can pursue careers as professional artists, designers, musicians, or actors, and may also work in art galleries, museums, and creative agencies.

Bachelor of Social Work (BSW)

Bachelor of Social Work (BSW) is designed for students interested in working in social services. This degree provides knowledge and skills in areas like community development, social justice, and welfare services. BSW graduates can work in NGOs, government agencies, and other organizations focused on improving social conditions.

Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA)

Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) is a popular choice for humanities students who want to enter the business world. This degree covers various aspects of business management, including marketing, finance, human resources, and operations. BBA graduates can work in corporate sectors, start their own business, or pursue an MBA for further specialization.

Bachelor of Mass Communication (BMC)

Bachelor of Mass Communication (BMC) is ideal for students interested in media and communication. This degree covers journalism, advertising, public relations, and electronic media. Graduates can work as journalists, content creators, public relations officers, and more in media houses, advertising agencies, and other communication-related fields.

Bachelor of Hotel Management (BHM)

Bachelor of Hotel Management (BHM) is perfect for students aspiring to work in the hospitality industry. This degree covers areas like hotel administration, food and beverage management, and event management. BHM graduates can work in hotels, resorts, cruise lines, and other hospitality-related businesses.

Bachelor of Education (B.Ed.)

Bachelor of Education (B.Ed.) is a professional degree for students who want to become teachers. This degree equips students with teaching skills and knowledge of educational theories and practices. B.Ed. graduates can work in schools, educational institutions, and even pursue higher education in the field of education.

Bachelor of Design (B.Des.)

Bachelor of Design (B.Des.) is suitable for students interested in design and creative fields. This degree offers specializations like fashion design, interior design, graphic design, and product design. B.Des. graduates can work as designers in various industries, including fashion, architecture, and media.

Career Options after Humanities

International Relations Specialist

Geography, Economics, Sociology, Political Science, History, Liberal Arts, and International Relations specialists may find it mentally stimulating. Graduate school to specialise in international relations follows a bachelor’s degree in one of these fields.

Understanding and managing global connections requires international relations specialists. They study political, economic, and social aspects that affect international relations. This aids wise decision-making and diplomatic strategies. The complex field of foreign relations requires more expertise as globalisation increases.

Salary: Entry-level positions may pay Rs. 50,000 per month, increasing with expertise and experience. High-level positions can earn up to Ten Lakhs per month.

Psychologist

A psychology or equivalent bachelor’s degree usually starts this job. Next, get a clinical, counselling, or industrial-organizational psychology masters. Anyone who follows this educational path will understand human behaviour, thoughts, and feelings better.

Psychology is essential in healthcare, education, business, and social services. They diagnose, treat, and study minds. Your salary in this sector varies on experience, specialisation, and employer.

Salary: Rs. 20,000–Rs. 250,000 per month. Clinical psychologists may earn more, and private practices can boost income.

Lawyer

Arts graduates find work easily. Law degrees are well-known. Law students can choose between a five-year integrated course or a three-year LL.B.

An integrated five-year plan usually comprises college and law school. Thus, students can start studying the law immediately. If they have an undergraduate degree in a related field or the arts, others can complete the three-year LL.B. Both paths lead to understanding the law, how things work, and moral issues.

Salary: Rs. 40,000–Rs. 45,000 per month. This depends on experience, expertise, and the law firm or organisation.

Foreign Language Expert

An artistic background could make a Foreign Language Expert employment intriguing. This field has fewer educational requirements than many others. You must enjoy languages and have a college degree to succeed in this job.

To succeed as a Foreign Language Expert, you must spend a lot of time with the language and study its culture and language. Although formal education isn’t confined to one field, it helps you learn and communicate in the selected language.

Salary: Average starting income is Rs. 30,000 per month, varying with employment and language ability.

Social Worker

People with artistic backgrounds can be happy in social work. Social workers help fix social issues and make cities healthier. Most people begin with a Bachelor’s and then a Master’s in Social Work. They gain information and skills.

Hospitals, schools, non-profits, and governments employ social workers. They defend children, the elderly, and the poor. Social workers improve society through psychotherapy, community aid, and community involvement.

Salary: Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 35,000 per month. Specialisation and experience can impact earnings.

Mass Communication

A humanities background makes mass communication rewarding. Many media jobs are available with a Bachelor’s degree in mass communication and a Post Graduate Diploma in a speciality. Mass communication comprises journalism, advertising, public relations, and radio. It lets people express themselves and communicate with others.

These educational milestones allow professionals to pursue careers as reporters, editors, content developers, or public relations experts. Experience and expertise determine salary in this field.

Salary: Starting salaries for entry-level jobs are normally between Rs. 15,000 and Rs. 20,000 per month, with potential for growth.

Hospitality Professional

After studying humanities, there are several employment options, including becoming a hotel manager with a bachelor’s degree. This area is dynamic and diversified, allowing people to develop while serving guests and guaranteeing a smooth experience.

A Bachelor of Hotel Management equips you for hospitality success. Hotel, cruise, and event management are rewarding jobs for graduates. Entry-level jobs typically pay Rs. 20,000 to Rs. 25,000 per month, with room for advancement as experience and competence increase.

Salary: Rs. 20,000 to Rs. 25,000 per month, with growth potential based on experience.
